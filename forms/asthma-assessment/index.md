# Asthma Assessment

Asthma control evaluation using the ACT (Asthma Control Test) scoring instrument.

## Scoring system

- **Instrument**: ACT Score (Asthma Control Test)
- **Range**: 5-25
- **Categories**: <=15 = Not well controlled, 16-19 = Not well controlled, 20-25 = Well controlled

## Steps

| #   | Step                 | Section                                                      |
| --- | -------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1   | Demographics         | Patient demographics and identification                      |
| 2   | Symptom Frequency    | Frequency of asthma symptoms in past 4 weeks                 |
| 3   | Lung Function        | Peak flow readings and spirometry results                    |
| 4   | Triggers             | Identified asthma triggers and exposure patterns             |
| 5   | Current Medications  | Controller and reliever medications with adherence           |
| 6   | Allergies            | Known allergies and allergic comorbidities                   |
| 7   | Exacerbation History | History of asthma attacks, ED visits, and hospitalisations   |
| 8   | Comorbidities        | Related conditions such as GERD, rhinitis, and obesity       |
| 9   | Social History       | Smoking status, occupational exposures, and home environment |
