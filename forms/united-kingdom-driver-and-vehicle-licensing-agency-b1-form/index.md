# United Kingdom Driver and Vehicle Licensing Agency B1 Form

Confidential medical information — neurological conditions for driving fitness assessment.

The DVLA B1 form is used by car drivers and motorcyclists in the United Kingdom to report neurological and brain-related medical conditions to the Driver and Vehicle Licensing Agency (DVLA). It covers brain haemorrhage, severe head injury, brain surgery, seizures/epilepsy, blackouts, medications, VP shunts, daily living assistance needs, double vision, eyesight problems, and vehicle adaptations.

## Source

- **Form**: B1 — Confidential medical information (neurological)
- **Agency**: Driver & Vehicle Licensing Agency (DVLA), United Kingdom
- **Revision**: May 2024 (Part A Rev May 24); Medical questionnaire Rev May 2023; Authorisation Rev March 2025
- **Pages**: 6
- **URL**: <https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/media/67ffcccc694d57c6b1cf8e2e/b1-confidential-medical-information.pdf>

## Conditions covered

- Acute/chronic subdural haematoma
- Aneurysm and arteriovenous malformation (AVM)
- Arachnoid cyst and Arnold-Chiari malformation
- Blood clots
- Brain abscess, cyst, encephalitis, and injury
- Burr hole surgery
- Cerebral palsy
- Head injuries (including serious)
- Hydrocephalus and hypoxic brain damage
- Lewy body dementia
- Subarachnoid haemorrhage
- Transient global amnesia
- VP shunt

### Medical Questionnaire — Neurological (Pages 2–4)

#### Question 1: Condition History

Please tick the appropriate box(es) if you have ever had any of the following (each with date DD/MM/YY):

- a) Brain haemorrhage (including subarachnoid, aneurysm & AVM) — checkbox + date + free text details
- b) Severe head injury involving in-patient treatment — checkbox + date + free text details
- c) Any other condition — checkbox + date + free text details
- d) Date of any brain surgery — date or "Not applicable"

#### Question 2: Treatment Provider

- Who did you last see for the treatment of this condition? GP / Consultant
- a) Please supply dates of consultations for this condition:
  - GP: Date of last contact (DD/MM/YY), Date of next contact (DD/MM/YY)
  - Consultant: Date of last contact (DD/MM/YY), Date of next contact (DD/MM/YY)

#### Question 3: Blackouts

Have you ever had a blackout(s)/altered level of consciousness?

- Yes / No
- If Yes, date (DD/MM/YY)

#### Question 4: Seizures/Epileptic Attacks

Have you ever had any form of seizures/epileptic attacks?

- Yes / No; if No → go to Q7
- If Yes, indicate diagnosis:
  - First ever seizure → go to Q5
  - More than one seizure ever or epilepsy → go to Q6

Note: "Epileptic attacks are variably described and involve fits, convulsions or seizures. Epilepsy may also occur only as auras strange feelings or taste, absences or blank spells, limb jerking or twitching. Epileptic episodes may occur when asleep or when awake."

#### Question 5: First Ever Seizure

- Date of the seizure (DD/MM/YY)
- Please give details (free text)

#### Question 6: More Than One Seizure or Epilepsy

- a) Have you ever had two or more seizures within a 5 year period? Yes / No
- b) First awake seizure — date (DD/MM/YY)
- c) First asleep seizure — date (DD/MM/YY)
- d) Last 2 awake seizures — dates (DD/MM/YY × 2)
- e) Last 2 asleep seizures — dates (DD/MM/YY × 2)
- f) If both awake and sleep attacks, date of first sleep attack after last awake attack — date
- g) Have your seizures ever affected your level of consciousness? Yes / No
  - If Yes → go to Q6h; if No → go to Q6i
- h) Would your seizures ever have caused difficulty controlling a vehicle? Yes / No
  - If No or Yes → give full description of the attack (free text)
- i) Was your last seizure a result of advice from your doctor to stop/reduce/change epilepsy medication? Yes / No
  - If No → go to Q6i; if Yes → go to Q6j:
    - (i) Date started to reduce/change medication (DD/MM/YY)
    - (ii) Has the previously effective medication been restarted? Yes / No
    - (iii) Date previously effective medication was restarted (DD/MM/YY)
    - (iv) Date of last seizure prior to medication withdrawal/reduction
- j) If seizure was provoked, provide details of circumstances and provoking factor (free text)

**Epilepsy Declaration** (required if diagnosis of epilepsy or more than 1 seizure):

I agree to:
- Follow the advice of my doctor(s) about treatment for this condition
- Attend where necessary, appointments to monitor my condition
- Inform DVLA should I experience any further attacks

Signed + Date

#### Question 7: Medication

Please give the name of any medication that you take/have taken (or "No medication taken"):

| Name of Medication | Start Date (DD/MM/YY) | End Date (DD/MM/YY) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| (up to 4 rows) | | |

- a) Does your medication make you drowsy or confused when driving? Yes / No

#### Question 8: VP Shunt

Have you ever had an insertion or upper end revision of a VP shunt or external ventricular drain?

- Yes / No
- If Yes, date (DD/MM/YY)

#### Question 9: Daily Living Assistance

Do you need help from another person with your day to day living?

- Yes / No
- If Yes, details of how they help you (free text)

#### Question 10: Double Vision (Diplopia)

- Yes / No; if No → go to Q11
- a) Is your double vision suppressed or controlled? Yes / No
- b) If Yes, how: Patch / Prism / Glasses/lenses / Other (free text)

#### Question 11: Eyesight Problems

Has your condition caused problems with your eyesight?

- Yes / No
- If Yes, details (free text)

#### Question 12: Vehicle Adaptations

Do you need to drive a vehicle fitted with special controls or automatic transmission?

- Yes / No; if No → skip Q12a and Q12b
- a) Have you told us before that you need special controls or automatic transmission? Yes / No; if Yes → answer Q12b
- b) Since your last licence was issued, have you had any additional controls fitted to your vehicle? Yes / No

Note: "If you have any relevant hospital notes about your medical condition, please send copies with this form."

### Applicant's Authorisation (Page 5)

Same structure as other DVLA forms (Rev March 2025):

- Declaration authorising medical information disclosure
- Criminal offence warning for false declarations
- Name, Signature, Date
- Electronic correspondence consent (email): Yes / No
- Contact preference from DVLA: Email / SMS (Text)
- Contact preference from healthcare professional on behalf of DVLA: Email / SMS (Text)

### Return Information (Page 6)

- **By Post**: Drivers Medical Group, DVLA, Swansea, SA99 1DF
- **By Email**: eftd@dvla.gov.uk
