# anesthesiology assessment

An anesthesiology assessment form (pre-operative evaluation) is a critical medical document used to evaluate a patient's health status before surgery, ensuring safety and optimizing the anesthetic plan. It covers medical history (allergies, previous complications, medications), physical exam (vitals, airway, heart/lungs), labs, and assigns an ASA Physical Status Class.

Key Components of an Anesthesiology Assessment Form

- Patient Demographics & Surgical Plan: Name, age, planned procedure, and proposed type of anesthesia.
- Medical History: Detailed review of cardiac, respiratory, neurological, and endocrine systems.
- Anesthetic History: Family history of Malignant Hyperthermia, previous anesthesia complications (e.g., nausea, awareness).
- Allergies & Medications: Current medications (specifically anticoagulants, antiplatelets, insulin).

Physical Examination:

- Airway Assessment: Mallampati score, mouth opening, neck movement, dentition (loose teeth).
- Vital Signs: BP, HR, SpO2, weight, and height.
- Systemic Review: Cardiovascular (murmurs) and Respiratory (wheezes, auscultation).
- Pre-op Investigations: Review of hemoglobin, electrolytes, renal function, ECG, and chest X-ray.
- Risk Stratification: Assignment of ASA Physical Status Class (I-VI) to assess operative risk.
- Anesthetic Plan: Plan for induction (e.g., Propofol), maintenance, pain management, and postoperative care.

Sample Forms and Tools

- General Pre-assessment Questionnaire: A patient-facing form covering medical history, lifestyle (alcohol, smoking), and daily activity limitations.
- Comprehensive PAC Form: A structured form for anesthesiologists covering physical exam findings, specific airway predictors, and medication management.
- Cardiac Risk Assessment: Often includes calculations like the Revised Cardiac Risk Index (RCRI/Lee Index).
