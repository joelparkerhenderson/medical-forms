This Code of Conduct for medical service providers reflects four fundamental principles: 

- (1) responsibilities to patients; 
- (2) respect for patients and colleagues; 
- (3) adherence to established medical standards;
- (4) an acknowledgement that medicine is an ever-evolving field that requires medical service providers to stay current in their fields to ensure excellent patient care. 
 
All medical service providers, locally employed staff, contractors, eligible family members, and re-employed annuitants, always uphold the following principles:

1. Ensure all individuals with eligibility for the Department's Medical program
   can access timely care.

2. Provide health care with competence and compassion.

3. Treat patients and colleagues with dignity and respect.

4. Ensure that all encounters are conducted without discriminating based on age,
   sexual orientation, gender, race, ethnicity, national origin, language,
   disease, disability, or religion.

5. Do not engage in retaliation against colleagues or patients for any reason.

6. Make all reasonable attempts to respectfully provide care to each patient. If
   for any reason a provider is unable to do so, ensure that the patient
   receives non-judgmental and prompt referral to another clinician or seek
   alternative options to ensure that patient autonomy is preserved.

7. Provide each patient with information needed to ensure considered
   decision-making about their health occurs. A patient's informed and voluntary
   consent should always be secured when possible before initiating a medical
   procedure.

8. Safeguard patient confidentiality, medical information, and privacy.

9. Report unintended events promptly, honestly, and thoroughly through
   appropriate channels.

10. Commit to continued education and adaptation, fulfill educational and
    training requirements, seek out and apply the soundest medical guidance, and
    in situations of uncertainty, ask for help.

11. Follow guidelines and policies with professionalism, honesty, and integrity.

12. Report wrongdoing (including violations of this Code of Coduct) to the appropriate authorities.
