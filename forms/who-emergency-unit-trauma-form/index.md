# WHO Emergency Unit Form: Trauma

World Health Organization standardised emergency unit clinical documentation form for trauma patients.

This comprehensive two-page form captures patient registration, initial vital signs, high risk red signs and high risk trauma indicators, triage category, ABCDE primary survey with detailed assessment findings and interventions, FAST ultrasound, history of present illness with injury mechanism details, past histories, full physical exam with body diagram, assessment and plan, diagnostics (labs and imaging), medications and procedures, reassessment vitals, and disposition/handover. Designed for use with the WHO Trauma Reference Card.

#### High Risk Red Signs

- **A/B**: Stridor, cyanosis, respiratory distress
- **C**: Poor perfusion, weak fast pulse, cap refill >3s, heavy bleeding; Adult HR <50 or >150; Child (≥2): lethargy, sunken eyes, slow skin pinch, poor drinking
- **D**: Unresponsive; Acute convulsions; Hypoglycaemia; Acute focal neurologic deficit; Altered mental status with fever or hypothermia or stiff neck or headache
- **Other**: Threatened limb; Snake bite; Poisoning, ingestion, chemical exposure; Violent or aggressive; Acute testicular pain or priapism; Adult severe chest or abdominal pain or ECG with ischemia; Pregnant with high risk findings; Infant <8 days old; Infant <2 months with temp >39°C or <36°C

#### High Risk Trauma

- **General Trauma**: Fall from twice person's height; All penetrating trauma (excepting distal to knee/elbow); Penetrating trauma distal to knee/elbow with uncontrolled bleeding; Crush injury; Polytrauma (injury to multiple body areas); Patient with bleeding disorder or on anticoagulation; Pregnant
- **Road Traffic**: High speed motor vehicle crash; Pedestrian or cyclist hit by vehicle; Other person in same vehicle died at scene; Motor vehicle crash without a seatbelt; Trapped or thrown from vehicle (including motorcycle); Dead on arrival

#### Primary Survey

**Treating Provider Assessment**: Date DD/MM/YY, Time (24h)

**A — Airway** (NML checkbox):
- Concerning findings: Swelling / Stridor / Voice changes / Burns / Obstructed by: Tongue / Blood / Secretion / Vomit / Foreign body
- Interventions: Repositioning / Suction / OPA / NPA / LMA / BVM / ETT
- Spine Stabilized: Done before arrival / Done in EU / Not needed

**B — Breathing** (NML checkbox):
- Spontaneous Respiratory Rate
- Chest Rise: Shallow / Retractions / Paradoxical
- Trachea: Midline / Deviated to L / R
- Breath Sounds: L__ / R__
- Cyanosis: Present on exam
- Interventions: Oxygen (L); Nasal / Facemask / NRB / BVM / CPAP/BIPAP / Ventilator
- Chest tube: Left - Size / Depth (cm); Right - Size / Depth (cm)

**C — Circulation** (NML checkbox):
- Skin: Warm / Dry / Cool / Moist / Pale
- Capillary refill: <3 sec or __ sec
- Pulses: Weak / Asymmetric
- JVD: Yes / No
- Unstable Pelvis: Yes / No
- Bleeding Controlled: Direct Pressure / Bandage / Tourniquet
- Access: IV / Central / IO — Line 1: Location / Size; Line 2: Location / Size
- Fluids: IVF __ mLs — NS / LR / Other
- Blood: Ordered / Given Type/Amount
- Pelvis Stabilized: Yes / Not Indicated

**D — Disability** (NML checkbox):
- Responsiveness: A / V / P / U
- GCS: __ (E__ V__ M__) / Qualified
- Moves Extremities: RUE / LUE / RLE / LLE
- Pupils: Size L__ R__; Reactivity L / R
- Blood Glucose (Abnormal if <65 mg/dL)
- Interventions: Glucose / Antidote / Antiepileptic / Raise head of bed / Other

**E — Exposure**: Exposed Completely (checkbox)

**F — FAST** (NML checkbox):
- Not Indicated / Not Available
- Peritoneum: Negative / Indeterminate / Free Fluid
- Chest: Negative / Indeterminate / Pneumothorax (R/L) / Pleural fluid (R/L) / Pericardial effusion

## Steps

| # | Step | Section |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Patient Registration | Demographics, arrival mode, residence, injury location |
| 2 | Chief Complaint & Vitals | Chief complaint, allergies, initial vital signs |
| 3 | High Risk Signs | Red signs (A/B, C, D, Other) and high risk trauma indicators |
| 4 | Triage | RED / YELLOW / GREEN category |
| 5 | Airway (A) | Primary survey airway assessment and interventions |
| 6 | Breathing (B) | Primary survey breathing assessment and interventions |
| 7 | Circulation (C) | Primary survey circulation, bleeding, access, fluids |
| 8 | Disability (D) | Neurological assessment, GCS, pupils, glucose |
| 9 | Exposure (E) & FAST (F) | Exposure and focused ultrasound |
| 10 | Injury History | Mechanism, intent, prehospital care, substance use |
| 11 | Past Histories | Medical, medications, surgeries, family history |
| 12 | Physical Exam | 11 body systems with body diagram |
| 13 | Assessment & Plan | Summary, differential, diagnostics plan |
| 14 | Diagnostics | Labs and imaging ordered/results |
| 15 | Medications & Procedures | Medications given, procedures performed |
| 16 | Reassessment | Repeat vital signs, condition change |
| 17 | Disposition | Discharge/admit/transfer, final vitals, provider signature |
