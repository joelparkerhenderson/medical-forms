World Health Organization standardised emergency unit clinical documentation form for trauma patients.

## Form sections

- **Patient Registration**: Demographics, arrival mode, residence, injury location
- **Chief Complaint & Vitals**: Complaint, allergies, initial VS, dead on arrival
- **High Risk Red Signs**: A/B, C, D, Other categories
- **High Risk Trauma**: General trauma and road traffic indicators
- **Triage**: RED / YELLOW / GREEN
- **Primary Survey (ABCDE + F)**: Airway, Breathing, Circulation, Disability, Exposure, FAST ultrasound
- **History of Present Illness**: Mechanism, intent, prehospital care, substance use
- **Past Histories**: Medical, medications, surgeries, family
- **Physical Exam**: 11 body systems with injury body diagram
- **Assessment & Plan**: Summary, differential, diagnostics plan
- **Diagnostics**: Labs (Hgb, Blood Type, Chemistry, Hepatic, UPT, Other) and Imaging (CXR, Pelvis XR, Head CT, C-spine, Chest/Abd CT, Extremity XR, Other)
- **Medications & Procedures**: Timed medication entries, procedure log
- **Reassessment**: Repeat vitals and condition status
- **Disposition**: Admit/Transfer/Discharge/Died, final VS, provider signature

## Key domain terms

- **FAST**: Focused Assessment with Sonography for Trauma
- **GCS**: Glasgow Coma Scale (E=Eye, V=Verbal, M=Motor)
- **AVPU**: Alert, Verbal, Pain, Unresponsive
- **Qualified GCS**: Patient sedated, intubated, or vision obstructed
- **Polytrauma**: Injury to multiple body areas
- **OPA/NPA/LMA/BVM/ETT**: Airway management devices
- **NRB**: Non-rebreather mask
- **IVF/NS/LR**: Intravenous fluid / Normal Saline / Lactated Ringer's
- **IO**: Intraosseous access
- **JVD**: Jugular venous distension
