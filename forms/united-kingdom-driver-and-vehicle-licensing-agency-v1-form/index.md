# United Kingdom Driver and Vehicle Licensing Agency V1 Form

General confidential medical information — vision self-declaration for driving fitness assessment.

The DVLA V1 form is used by drivers in the United Kingdom to declare vision-related medical conditions to the Driver and Vehicle Licensing Agency (DVLA). It collects personal details, healthcare professional information, and a comprehensive vision self-declaration questionnaire covering eyesight standards, monocular vision, visual field problems, glaucoma, retinitis pigmentosa, laser treatment, blepharospasm, night blindness, double vision, and other vision conditions. The form includes an applicant's authorisation section for medical information disclosure.

## Source

- **Agency**: Driver & Vehicle Licensing Agency (DVLA), United Kingdom
- **Revision**: September 2025 (V1 Rev Sept 25); Authorisation section Rev July 2022

### Vision Self-Declaration (Pages 2–5)

#### Question 1: Eyesight Standards

Eyesight must not be worse than 6/12 (decimal 0.5) on the Snellen scale. Must be able to read a number plate from 20 metres (~5 parked cars length), with or without glasses/corrective lenses.

- a) Do you meet the eyesight standard for driving?
  - Yes, without glasses or corrective lenses
  - Yes, with glasses or corrective lenses
  - No

#### Question 2: Vision in Both Eyes

Do you have vision in both eyes? (Do not include long or short sightedness.)

- Yes → go to Q3
- No:
  - a) Which eye: Left eye only / Right eye only
  - b) How long: Since birth or childhood / As a result of a health condition, accident or injury
  - c) Monocular declaration: Confirm adapted to vision in one eye (advised by healthcare professional or not)

#### Question 3: Field of Vision

Have you been told by your healthcare professional or optician/optometrist that you have a problem with your field of vision? (Total area visible when looking straight ahead, including side vision.)

- a) Yes / No → go to Q4
- b) If Yes, is it caused solely by an eye condition? Yes → go to Q4 / No
- c) If No, cause: Brain tumour / Head injury / Stroke / Other (free text)

#### Question 4: Glaucoma

- Yes / No → go to Q5
- a) Which eye(s): Both eyes / Left eye / Right eye

#### Question 5: Retinitis Pigmentosa

- Yes / No → go to Q6
- a) Which eye(s): Both eyes / Left eye / Right eye

#### Question 6: Laser Treatment

Have you had laser treatment? (Do not include treatment for long/short sightedness or cataracts.)

- Yes / No → go to Q7
- a) Date of first treatment: Left eye (date) / Right eye (date)
- b) Date of last treatment: Left eye (date) / Right eye (date)

#### Question 7: Blepharospasm

- Yes / No → go to Q8
- a) Which eye(s): Both eyes / Left eye / Right eye
- b) Have you had any treatment? Yes / No
- c) Is your healthcare professional satisfied it is adequately controlled? Yes / No

#### Question 8: Night Blindness (Nyctalopia)

- Yes / No → go to Q9
- a) Which eye(s): Both eyes / Left eye / Right eye

#### Question 9: Double Vision (Diplopia)

- Yes / No → go to Q10
- a) If Yes, is it controlled? Yes / No
- b) If No, has it been the same for 6 months or more? Yes / No
- c) Double vision declaration: Adaptation period of 3+ months required when driving with patch, prism, frosted glasses, or lenses (ability to judge distances affected; may not be aware of objects on each side). Confirmation checkbox and signature with date.

#### Question 10: Other Vision Condition

- Yes / No → go to Q11
- a) Free text: condition(s) and which eye(s) affected

#### Question 11: Recent Contact with Healthcare Professional

Have you had any contact (telephone, video, or face to face) with your healthcare professional or optician/optometrist about your vision condition in the last 12 months?

- Yes / No
- a) If Yes, date of contact (DD/MM/YY)

### Applicant's Authorisation (Page 6)

Important information about fitness to drive:

- DVLA may require medical examination and/or practical assessment
- Individuals involved may include doctors, orthoptists at eye clinics, or paramedical staff at a driving assessment centre
- Only fitness-to-drive-relevant medical information is shared
- Relevant medical information may be considered by the Secretary of State's Honorary Medical Advisory Panels (confidential)

**Declaration** (must NOT be altered):

- Authorises doctor/specialist/healthcare professional to disclose medical information to DVLA on behalf of the Secretary of State for Transport
- Authorisation may be passed to another registered healthcare professional
- Secretary of State may disclose information to doctors, orthoptists, paramedical staff, and Honorary Medical Advisory panel members
- Declares details are correct to best of knowledge and belief
- Acknowledges false declaration is a criminal offence
