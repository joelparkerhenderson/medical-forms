# WHO Emergency Unit Form: General

World Health Organization standardised emergency unit clinical documentation form for general (non-trauma) patients.

- **Organisation**: World Health Organization (WHO)

## Form sections

- **Patient Registration**: Demographics, arrival mode, ambulance timestamps, ambulatory status
- **Chief Complaint & Vitals**: Complaint, triage, initial VS, pain scale
- **High Risk Signs**: Abnormal AVPU, HR, stridor, perfusion, temp, SpO2, respiratory distress
- **Primary Survey (ABCD)**: Airway, Breathing, Circulation, Disability with interventions
- **History of Present Illness**: Symptoms, time course, prior episodes and interventions
- **Review of Systems**: 14 systems (General, HEENT, Resp, CV, GI, Pelvis/GU/Rectal, Female Reproductive, Male Reproductive, Skin, MSK, Heme, Neuro, Psychiatric, Pediatric Specific)
- **Past Medical History**: Medications, allergies, pregnancy, substance use, surgical history, family history
- **Physical Exam**: 11 body systems (General, Neuro/Psych, HEENT, Neck, Respiratory, Cardiac, Abdominal, Pelvis/GU/Rectal, Lymph, MSK, Skin)
- **Diagnostics**: CBC diagram, lytes diagram, UPT, malaria, HIV, blood type, urine dip, ECG, imaging
- **Additional Interventions**: Medications/fluids and procedures with times
- **Assessment & Plan**: Summary, differential, plan
- **Reassessment**: Repeat vitals
- **Disposition**: Admit/Transfer/Discharge/Died, final VS, provider signature

## Assessment steps (16 total)

1. Patient Registration
2. Chief Complaint & Vitals
3. High Risk Signs
4. Airway (A)
5. Breathing (B)
6. Circulation (C)
7. Disability (D)
8. History of Present Illness
9. Review of Systems
10. Past Medical History
11. Physical Exam
12. Diagnostics
13. Additional Interventions
14. Assessment & Plan
15. Reassessment
16. Disposition

## Key differences from Trauma form

- No FAST ultrasound section
- No injury mechanism/intent section
- Has Review of Systems (14 systems) — trauma form does not
- Has detailed diagnostics layout with CBC/lytes diagram and ECG interpretation
- Includes ambulance level (Basic/Advanced) and emergency system timestamps
- Tracks ambulatory vs non-ambulatory and acute vs chronic
- Includes Lymph in physical exam (trauma has Pelvis and GU/Rectal separately)

## Key domain terms

- **AVPU**: Alert, Verbal, Pain, Unresponsive
- **GCS**: Glasgow Coma Scale
- **CBC**: Complete Blood Count (WBC, Hgb, Plt, Hct)
- **Lytes**: Electrolytes (Na, K, Cl, HCO3, BUN, Cr, Glucose)
- **UPT**: Urine Pregnancy Test
- **CVL**: Central Venous Line
- **MUAC**: Mid-Upper Arm Circumference
- **CVA**: Costovertebral Angle
