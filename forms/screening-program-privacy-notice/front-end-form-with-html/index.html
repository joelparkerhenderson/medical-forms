Screening Programme Privacy Notice

Please read this notice carefully before confirming below

Medical research and service planning

uses and shares information from your medical records to support medical research and to help plan NHS services.

Information is shared with and national NHS organisations, either with your explicit consent or where the law allows us to do so.

We do this because it is important for:

  • developing new treatments and medicines;
  • improving care for patients with specific conditions;
  • planning NHS services to meet the needs of the local population;
  • understanding patterns of disease across the country.

Only the minimum necessary information is used, and it is shared only with organisations that have a legal basis to receive it.

Right to object

You have the right to object to your information being used for medical research and service planning. To do so, please speak to a member of staff at this practice, or visit nhs.uk/your-nhs-data-matters to register a national data opt-out.

Checking quality of care — national clinical audits

contributes to national clinical audits, which are used to check and improve the quality of care provided to patients across the NHS.

Data is sent to for this purpose. The information shared may include your NHS Number, date of birth, and coded health information about your care.

Information is only shared with national audit bodies where the law allows us to do so.

Right to object

You can register a national data opt-out to prevent your information being shared for national clinical audits. However, in some circumstances — for example, where there is a patient safety concern — your information may still need to be shared.

Visit nhs.uk/your-nhs-data-matters to register a national data opt-out, or speak to the practice.

National screening programmes

The NHS provides national screening programmes so that certain diseases can be detected at an early stage. These screening programmes include bowel cancer, breast cancer, cervical cancer, aortic aneurysms, and a diabetic eye screening service.

The law allows us to share your contact information with the UK Health Security Agency, or other relevant health organisations, so that you can be invited to the appropriate screening programmes.

You can opt out of receiving invitations for screening programmes. You may want to discuss this with your GP.

There are different options for opting out of the different screening programmes. Please visit: gov.uk — opting out of screening

More information can be found at: gov.uk — population screening programmes or speak to the practice.

Legal information

We are required by law to provide you with the following information about how we handle your information.

Data Controller

Data Protection Officer

Purpose of the processing

  • The NHS provides several national health screening programmes to detect diseases or conditions early, such as cervical and breast cancer, aortic aneurysm, and diabetes.
  • Medical research and planning of NHS services.
  • Checking the quality of care through national clinical audit.
  • Sharing information so that the correct people are invited for screening — meaning those most at risk can be offered treatment.

Lawful basis for processing

  • Article 6(1)(e) — processing is necessary for the performance of a task carried out in the exercise of official authority vested in the controller.
  • Article 9(2)(a) — the data subject has given explicit consent to the processing of those personal data for one or more specified purposes.
  • Article 9(2)(j) — processing is necessary for archiving purposes in the public interest, scientific or historical research purposes, or statistical purposes.
  • Article 9(2)(h) — processing is necessary for the purposes of preventative medicine, the provision of health or social care or treatment, or the management of health or social care systems and services (for national clinical audit and screening).

Recipients of the processed data

  • (medical research and service planning)
  • (planning NHS services)
  • (national clinical audit)
  • UK Health Security Agency and other relevant health organisations (national screening programmes)

Right to object and the National Data Opt-out

You have the right to object to your information being used for medical research and service planning, and for national clinical audit. You can register a national data opt-out (NDOO) at nhs.uk/your-nhs-data-matters.

You can also ask this practice to apply a Type 1 opt-out to prevent your information from being shared outside this practice for purposes beyond your direct care.

For national screening programmes, you can opt out so that you no longer receive an invitation. See: gov.uk — opting out of screening, or speak to your practice.

Right of access and right to correct

  • You have the right to access your medical record. Please speak to a member of staff or see our subject access request policy:
  • You have the right to correct personal information which is inaccurate or a mistake. Please speak to a member of staff.
  • We are not aware of any circumstances in which you will have the right to delete correct information from your medical record, although you are free to obtain your own legal advice if you believe there is no lawful purpose for which we hold the information and contact us if you hold a different view.

Right to restriction of processing

You have the right to ask us to restrict the processing of your personal information in certain circumstances. Please contact us if you would like to make a request.

Retention period

GP medical records will be kept in line with the law and national guidance. Information on how long records can be kept can be found at: NHS Records Management Code, or speak to the practice.

Right to complain

You have the right to complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO): https://ico.org.uk/global/contact-us/ or call 0303 123 1113.

Data we receive from other organisations

We receive information about your health from other organisations who are involved in providing you with health and social care. For example, if you go to hospital for treatment, the hospital will send us a letter to let us know what happened. This means your GP medical record is kept up to date when you receive care from other parts of the health service.
