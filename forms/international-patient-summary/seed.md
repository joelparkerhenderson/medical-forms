The International Patient Summary (IPS) is a standardized, minimal, and electronic health record extract designed for cross-border or emergency care. It provides essential clinical information—such as allergies, medications, and medical history—to clinicians, ensuring safe, continuous care, particularly for unplanned, international medical situations. 

Key Aspects of the IPS:
Purpose: Facilitates patient care across borders, allowing health professionals access to vital information when treating patients from different countries.
Content: Contains a minimal, non-exhaustive, specialty-agnostic dataset including medications, allergies, past surgeries, and immunizations.
Standards: Built on FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources) standards to ensure interoperability.
Initiatives: G7 countries are working on adopting the IPS to allow patients to view, download, and share their data, with an audit trail of access.

Core Purpose and Use Cases
Unscheduled Care: Provides clinicians with vital facts during emergencies or accidents when a patient is away from home.
Global Interoperability: Uses open standards to allow different health IT systems around the world to exchange and understand the data.
Minimal & Non-Exhaustive: It is not a full medical record; instead, it contains only the most relevant, "specialty-agnostic" data needed for safe care. 

What Data Does it Include?
The IPS dataset typically focuses on a core set of clinical information: 

Medications: Current prescriptions and lists.
Allergies: Known reactions to drugs or other substances.
Medical Problems: Major diagnoses or chronic conditions.
Procedures: Significant past surgeries or medical interventions.
Demographics: Basic identity details like name, address, and date of birth. 
Technical Standards
The IPS is built on internationally recognised technical frameworks to ensure it works across different countries: 

HL7 FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources): The primary standard used to format the data for electronic exchange.
SNOMED CT: Provides a standardised clinical terminology so that medical terms mean the same thing in different languages.
ISO 27269: The formal international standard that defines the IPS data set. 

Adoption and Availability
G7 and Global Initiatives: Several countries, including the G7 nations, have committed to adopting the IPS standard to improve global health data sharing.
Europe (MyHealth@EU): The IPS is gaining significant traction in Europe to support cross-border patient summary exchanges.
UK Context: While the NHS England has developed proof-of-concept demonstrations for IPS, it is distinct from the domestic Summary Care Record (SCR), which is used for care within England.
