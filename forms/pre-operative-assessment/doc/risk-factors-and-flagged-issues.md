# Risk Factors and Flagged Issues

This document details all safety-critical flags generated by the Pre-Operative Assessment system. These flags are designed to alert the reviewing anaesthetist to conditions requiring specific attention, preparation, or intervention, independent of the ASA grade.

## Flag Priority System

Flags are classified into three priority levels:

| Priority | Meaning | Display | Action Expected |
|----------|---------|---------|-----------------|
| **High** | Immediate safety concern; requires specific preparation or protocol activation | Red banner, top of list | Must be acknowledged before proceeding |
| **Medium** | Significant clinical consideration; influences anaesthetic plan | Yellow/amber banner | Should be reviewed and documented |
| **Low** | Notable finding; for information and documentation | Grey banner | Document in anaesthetic chart |

Flags are always sorted by priority (high first) in both the on-screen report and PDF output.

## Complete Flag Catalogue

### Airway Flags

Airway management is the single most critical safety domain in anaesthesia. Failure to anticipate a difficult airway is a leading cause of anaesthesia-related morbidity and mortality.

| Flag ID | Category | Message | Priority | Trigger |
|---------|----------|---------|----------|---------|
| FLAG-AIRWAY-001 | Airway | Previous difficult airway reported | High | Previous difficult airway = yes |
| FLAG-AIRWAY-002 | Airway | Limited mouth opening | High | Limited mouth opening = yes |
| FLAG-AIRWAY-003 | Airway | Limited neck movement | High | Limited neck movement = yes |
| FLAG-AIRWAY-004 | Airway | Mallampati Class 3 or 4 | High | Mallampati score = 3 or 4 |
| FLAG-AIRWAY-005 | Airway | Cervical spine issues - consider C-spine precautions | Medium | Cervical spine issues = yes |
| FLAG-AIRWAY-006 | Airway | Rheumatoid arthritis - assess atlanto-axial stability | Medium | Rheumatoid arthritis = yes |
| FLAG-DENTAL-001 | Airway | Dental issues (with details) | Low | Dental issues = yes |

**Clinical Context:**
- **FLAG-AIRWAY-001** is the most critical airway flag. A patient with a documented difficult airway history should trigger a difficult airway management plan including equipment preparation, senior anaesthetist involvement, and consideration of awake fibreoptic intubation.
- **FLAG-AIRWAY-002/003** (limited mouth opening and neck movement) are independent predictors of difficult intubation. When both are present, the risk is substantially increased.
- **FLAG-AIRWAY-004** Mallampati class 3-4 predicts more difficult laryngoscopy. Sensitivity and positive predictive value are moderate when used alone, but improve significantly when combined with other airway assessments.
- **FLAG-AIRWAY-006** Rheumatoid arthritis can cause atlanto-axial subluxation, potentially leading to spinal cord compression during neck extension for intubation. Flexion-extension cervical spine X-rays may be indicated.

### Allergy Flags

| Flag ID | Category | Message | Priority | Trigger |
|---------|----------|---------|----------|---------|
| FLAG-ALLERGY-ANAPH-{allergen} | Allergy | ANAPHYLAXIS history: {allergen name} | High | Any allergy with severity = anaphylaxis |
| FLAG-ALLERGY-001 | Allergy | {N} allergy/allergies documented | Medium | Any allergies present |

**Clinical Context:**
- Anaphylaxis flags are generated per allergen, ensuring each anaphylactic allergy is individually highlighted.
- Common perioperative allergens include latex, antibiotics (penicillin, cephalosporins), muscle relaxants (suxamethonium, atracurium), NSAIDs, and chlorhexidine.
- The operating theatre should be prepared with allergy-safe alternatives and anaphylaxis treatment immediately available.

### Anticoagulation and Haematological Flags

| Flag ID | Category | Message | Priority | Trigger |
|---------|----------|---------|----------|---------|
| FLAG-ANTICOAG-001 | Anticoagulation | On anticoagulants: {type} | High | Anticoagulants = yes |
| FLAG-HAEM-001 | Haematological | Bleeding disorder reported | High | Bleeding disorder = yes |
| FLAG-SICKLE-001 | Haematological | Sickle cell disease - avoid hypoxia, hypothermia, dehydration | High | Sickle cell disease = yes |

**Clinical Context:**
- **FLAG-ANTICOAG-001** is one of the most operationally important flags. Anticoagulant management requires:
  - Identification of the specific agent (warfarin, rivaroxaban, apixaban, dabigatran, enoxaparin, clopidogrel, aspirin, ticagrelor, etc.)
  - Assessment of bleeding vs. thrombotic risk
  - Decision on withholding timing (varies by agent: warfarin 5 days, DOACs 24-72 hours, clopidogrel 7 days)
  - Bridging anticoagulation if indicated
  - INR/anti-Xa level checking if appropriate
- **FLAG-HAEM-001** Bleeding disorders require haematology input for factor replacement planning, desmopressin consideration, and surgical haemostasis strategy.
- **FLAG-SICKLE-001** The specific perioperative precautions listed (avoid hypoxia, hypothermia, dehydration) are evidence-based interventions to prevent sickle cell crisis.

### Malignant Hyperthermia and Anaesthetic History Flags

| Flag ID | Category | Message | Priority | Trigger |
|---------|----------|---------|----------|---------|
| FLAG-MH-001 | Malignant Hyperthermia | Family history of malignant hyperthermia - AVOID TRIGGERS | High | Family MH history = yes |
| FLAG-ANAES-001 | Previous Anaesthesia | Previous anaesthesia problems: {details} | High | Anaesthesia problems = yes |

**Clinical Context:**
- **FLAG-MH-001** is potentially the most life-threatening flag in the system. Malignant hyperthermia (MH) is a pharmacogenetic condition triggered by volatile anaesthetic agents (sevoflurane, desflurane, isoflurane) and depolarising muscle relaxants (suxamethonium). The response includes:
  - Total intravenous anaesthesia (TIVA) must be used
  - MH-free anaesthetic machine preparation (flush with oxygen for 20+ minutes, remove/disable vaporisers)
  - Dantrolene must be immediately available
  - Prolonged post-operative monitoring
- **FLAG-ANAES-001** captures any previous anaesthetic problems. These could range from difficult intubation to awareness, prolonged paralysis, or severe allergic reactions.

### Cardiac Flags

| Flag ID | Category | Message | Priority | Trigger |
|---------|----------|---------|----------|---------|
| FLAG-CARDIAC-001 | Cardiac | Pacemaker/ICD in situ - check device, magnet availability | High | Pacemaker = yes |
| FLAG-CARDIAC-002 | Cardiac | Recent MI ({N} weeks ago) | High | Recent MI = yes |

**Clinical Context:**
- **FLAG-CARDIAC-001** Pacemaker/ICD patients require:
  - Device interrogation pre-operatively (check battery, mode, thresholds)
  - Magnet availability in theatre (to switch ICD to pacing-only mode during diathermy)
  - Bipolar diathermy preference, diathermy pad placement away from device
  - Post-operative device check
- **FLAG-CARDIAC-002** Recent MI timing is critical for surgical risk stratification. Current guidelines suggest:
  - < 30 days: Very high risk, defer all but life-saving surgery
  - 30-60 days: High risk, defer elective surgery
  - > 60 days: Proceed with appropriate monitoring

### Aspiration Risk Flags

| Flag ID | Category | Message | Priority | Trigger |
|---------|----------|---------|----------|---------|
| FLAG-GI-001 | Aspiration Risk | GORD/hiatus hernia - aspiration risk, consider RSI | Medium | GORD = yes OR hiatus hernia = yes |

**Clinical Context:**
- Patients with GORD or hiatus hernia have increased risk of passive regurgitation during anaesthesia.
- Rapid sequence induction (RSI) with cricoid pressure may be indicated.
- Pre-operative proton pump inhibitor (PPI) or H2-receptor antagonist prophylaxis should be considered.
- Sodium citrate may be given as a buffer for gastric acid.

### Respiratory Flags

| Flag ID | Category | Message | Priority | Trigger |
|---------|----------|---------|----------|---------|
| FLAG-OSA-001 | Respiratory | OSA (on/no CPAP) - post-op monitoring | Medium | OSA = yes |
| FLAG-URTI-001 | Respiratory | Recent URTI - consider postponing elective surgery | Medium | Recent URTI = yes |

**Clinical Context:**
- **FLAG-OSA-001** OSA patients have increased sensitivity to opioids and sedatives, higher risk of airway obstruction, and may need post-operative HDU/ICU monitoring. CPAP compliance status is noted.
- **FLAG-URTI-001** Recent URTI (within 2-4 weeks) increases airway reactivity and risk of laryngospasm, bronchospasm, and oxygen desaturation. Elective surgery should generally be postponed by 2-4 weeks.

### Pregnancy Flags

| Flag ID | Category | Message | Priority | Trigger |
|---------|----------|---------|----------|---------|
| FLAG-PREG-001 | Pregnancy | Confirmed pregnancy ({N} weeks) / Possible pregnancy - confirm before proceeding | High | Possibly pregnant = yes |

**Clinical Context:**
- Pregnancy has profound implications for anaesthetic management:
  - Teratogenic risk of drugs (especially first trimester)
  - Altered pharmacokinetics (increased cardiac output, reduced protein binding)
  - Aspiration risk (progesterone-mediated gastric relaxation)
  - Aortocaval compression in late pregnancy (left lateral tilt required)
  - Fetal monitoring requirements
  - Availability of obstetric and neonatal teams
- If pregnancy is possible but not confirmed, a pregnancy test should be performed before proceeding.

### Endocrine Flags

| Flag ID | Category | Message | Priority | Trigger |
|---------|----------|---------|----------|---------|
| FLAG-INSULIN-001 | Endocrine | On insulin - perioperative glucose management plan needed | Medium | Diabetes on insulin = yes |
| FLAG-ADRENAL-001 | Endocrine | Adrenal insufficiency - stress-dose steroids may be required | High | Adrenal insufficiency = yes |

**Clinical Context:**
- **FLAG-INSULIN-001** Insulin-dependent diabetic patients require a variable-rate insulin infusion (VRII / "sliding scale") protocol perioperatively, with regular blood glucose monitoring.
- **FLAG-ADRENAL-001** Patients on chronic steroids or with adrenal insufficiency cannot mount an appropriate cortisol response to surgical stress. Without supplementation, they risk adrenal crisis (hypotension, cardiovascular collapse). Typical stress dosing: hydrocortisone 25-100mg IV at induction depending on surgical severity.

### Procedural Flags

| Flag ID | Category | Message | Priority | Trigger |
|---------|----------|---------|----------|---------|
| FLAG-EMERG-001 | Procedure | Emergency procedure - limited time for optimisation | High | Procedure urgency = emergency |

**Clinical Context:**
- Emergency procedures carry inherently higher risk due to:
  - Limited time for pre-operative investigation and optimisation
  - Patients often not fasted (aspiration risk)
  - Acute physiological derangement
  - Reduced availability of senior staff (out-of-hours)
  - Inability to defer surgery for risk reduction

## Flag Interactions and Compound Risk

Certain flag combinations indicate particularly high-risk scenarios:

| Combination | Significance |
|---|---|
| FLAG-AIRWAY-001 + FLAG-AIRWAY-002 + FLAG-AIRWAY-003 | Multiple difficult airway predictors: awake fibreoptic intubation strongly indicated |
| FLAG-MH-001 + FLAG-EMERG-001 | MH-susceptible patient for emergency surgery: immediate MH-safe preparation needed |
| FLAG-ANTICOAG-001 + FLAG-EMERG-001 | Anticoagulated patient for emergency surgery: reversal agents may be needed |
| FLAG-PREG-001 + FLAG-EMERG-001 | Pregnant patient for emergency surgery: obstetric and neonatal standby |
| FLAG-CARDIAC-002 + any surgical flag | Recent MI: escalation to senior anaesthetist and cardiology input |
| FLAG-ADRENAL-001 + FLAG-EMERG-001 | Adrenal insufficiency with emergency surgery: immediate stress-dose steroids |

The system presents all flags together on the report, allowing the clinician to identify these compound risk scenarios.

## Flag vs. ASA Rule Relationship

Flags and ASA rules serve different purposes:

| Aspect | ASA Rules | Additional Flags |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Purpose** | Grade the patient's overall fitness | Alert to specific safety concerns |
| **Output** | Single numerical grade (I-V) | Multiple individual alerts |
| **Scope** | Overall comorbidity burden | Specific actionable findings |
| **Overlap** | Some conditions trigger both a rule and a flag | Many flags have no corresponding ASA rule |

For example, a pacemaker triggers ASA rule CV-010 (grade II) AND flag FLAG-CARDIAC-001. The rule contributes to the grade; the flag ensures the specific device management requirements are not overlooked.

Conversely, dental issues generate flag FLAG-DENTAL-001 but do not trigger any ASA rule, as they do not affect the overall physical status classification but are important for airway management.
