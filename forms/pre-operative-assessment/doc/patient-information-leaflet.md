# Patient Information: Your Pre-Operative Assessment

## What is a pre-operative assessment?

Before you have your operation, we need to understand your general health so that we can plan the safest possible anaesthetic for you. A pre-operative assessment is a health check that helps us:

- Understand your medical conditions
- Check which medicines you take
- Know about any allergies you have
- Assess how fit you are for your operation
- Plan the best type of anaesthetic for you
- Identify anything that needs special attention

## Why am I being asked to fill in a questionnaire?

This digital questionnaire helps us collect all the important information about your health in a structured way. Your answers will be reviewed by a qualified anaesthetist (the doctor who will look after you during your operation) who will use them to plan your care.

The questionnaire is **not** a replacement for seeing a doctor. It is a tool to help us prepare for your consultation and ensure nothing important is missed.

## What questions will I be asked?

The questionnaire covers 16 sections about different aspects of your health:

1. **Your basic details** - name, date of birth, weight, height
2. **Heart and circulation** - blood pressure, heart conditions
3. **Lungs and breathing** - asthma, COPD, sleep apnoea, smoking
4. **Kidneys** - kidney disease, dialysis
5. **Liver** - liver problems, hepatitis
6. **Hormones** - diabetes, thyroid conditions
7. **Brain and nerves** - stroke, epilepsy
8. **Blood** - bleeding problems, blood thinners
9. **Joints and airway** - neck movement, jaw opening, dental work
10. **Stomach and digestion** - acid reflux, hiatus hernia
11. **Medications** - all medicines you currently take
12. **Allergies** - any allergies to medicines, foods, or other substances
13. **Previous anaesthetics** - any problems with anaesthesia before
14. **Lifestyle** - alcohol and other substances
15. **Fitness level** - how much exercise you can do
16. **Pregnancy** - if applicable (women of childbearing age only)

## How long will it take?

Most people complete the questionnaire in **10 to 15 minutes**. Take your time and answer as accurately as you can. There is no time limit.

## How do I prepare?

To answer the questions accurately, it helps to:

- **Bring a list of your current medications** (or bring the medicines themselves). Include any over-the-counter medicines, supplements, or herbal remedies.
- **Know your allergies**, including what reaction you had (e.g., rash, swelling, difficulty breathing).
- **Think about your medical history**, including any hospital admissions, operations, or diagnoses.
- **Know about any family medical history** that may be relevant, especially if any family members have had problems with anaesthesia.

If you are unsure about any question, it is better to mention something than to leave it out. The anaesthetist can discuss any unclear points with you afterwards.

## What happens to my answers?

Your answers are used to:

1. **Generate a risk assessment** (called an ASA grade) that helps the anaesthetist understand your overall health status.
2. **Highlight specific issues** that need special attention during your operation, such as allergies, blood-thinning medicines, or airway concerns.
3. **Create a report** that goes into your medical record and helps the surgical team plan your care.

### Your privacy

- Your answers are **only used for planning your anaesthetic and surgery**.
- The information is **not stored on the tablet or computer** after your session ends.
- A PDF report is generated for your medical record.
- Your information is handled in accordance with data protection regulations.

## What is an ASA grade?

The ASA grade is a standard classification used worldwide to describe a patient's general health before an operation:

| Grade | What it means |
|-------|--------------|
| **ASA I** | You are a healthy person with no significant medical conditions |
| **ASA II** | You have a mild medical condition that is well controlled (e.g., well-managed blood pressure, mild asthma) |
| **ASA III** | You have a more serious medical condition that affects your daily life (e.g., poorly controlled diabetes, moderate lung disease) |
| **ASA IV** | You have a very serious medical condition (e.g., recent heart attack, severe heart failure) |

A higher ASA grade does not mean you cannot have surgery. It means the anaesthetist will take extra precautions to keep you safe.

## What if I do not understand a question?

- You can ask a member of staff for help at any time.
- If you are unsure about a medical term, ask the nurse or receptionist to explain it.
- You can go back to any previous question using the "Previous" button.
- If you do not know the answer, it is fine to leave it blank - the anaesthetist will ask you about it during your consultation.

## What if I make a mistake?

You can go back to any section and change your answers before submitting. Use the "Previous" button to navigate back through the questionnaire.

## What happens after I submit the questionnaire?

1. **Your answers are used to generate a report** summarising your health information and any areas of concern.
2. **An anaesthetist will review the report** before or on the day of your operation.
3. **You may be contacted** if additional information, tests, or consultations are needed before your surgery.
4. **On the day of your operation**, the anaesthetist will discuss your anaesthetic plan with you and answer any questions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I have to fill in the questionnaire digitally?**
A: If you prefer, a paper version is available. Please ask a member of staff.

**Q: Can someone help me fill it in?**
A: Yes. A family member, carer, or member of staff can help you complete the questionnaire.

**Q: What if my health changes between filling this in and my operation?**
A: Please let your surgical team know about any changes to your health, especially new diagnoses, changes to your medicines, or new symptoms.

**Q: Will the anaesthetist only use this questionnaire?**
A: No. The anaesthetist will also review your medical records, examine you if needed, and discuss any concerns with you directly. The questionnaire helps but does not replace the clinical assessment.

**Q: What if I am worried about my ASA grade?**
A: The ASA grade is a tool for the medical team, not a judgement on you. A higher grade simply means the team will take additional steps to ensure your safety. If you have concerns, please discuss them with your anaesthetist.

**Q: I take lots of medications - do I need to list them all?**
A: Yes, please list all medications including prescriptions, over-the-counter medicines, vitamins, and supplements. This helps the anaesthetist identify any potential interactions with anaesthetic drugs.

**Q: Why are you asking about my family's medical history?**
A: Some conditions that affect anaesthesia can run in families, particularly malignant hyperthermia (a rare reaction to certain anaesthetic drugs). Knowing your family history helps us take appropriate precautions.
